Where is it ?

Urubamba (9,420 ft.) is in the Sacred Valley, a one hour drive from Cusco (11,152 ft.) in the Andes of Peru. Cusco is a 45 minute flight from Peru’s capital, Lima, on the Pacific coast.
The Sacred Valley is a collection of towns along the Urubamba river. It’s is packed with cool things to see; most notably, Machu Picchu

Flying there

To get to the Sacred Valley you have to fly into Cusco from Lima. Many airlines have either one stop or direct flights from the U.S. to Lima. A non-stop flight from Atlanta to Lima takes about 6.5 hours.
Recommended Lima – Cusco airlines are LATAM, TACA (Avianca), LCP and Star Peru.

Getting from Cusco to Urubamba

Your hotel should be able to set airport pick up for you, if not, we can help you with that (just send us an email).
A trip from the Cusco airport to Urubamba should be between $20 to $30 for a sedan.
We do not recommend renting a car unless you are really into adventure driving.

Transport and paying for stuff

Once in the Valley, it’s easy to set private transportation. For short trips you can also use local moto-taxis (tuk-tuk).
Although you can pay with credit card for most big purchases (hotel, nice meals,etc), it’s better to have cash for the local services (transport, markets). Exchanging dollars to soles is readily available and about 3.3 soles per dollar
