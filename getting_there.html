Where is it ?

The town of Urubamba (9420 Feet ASL) is located in the Sacred Valley of the Incas, a one-hour drive from the city of Cuzco (11152 Feet ASL) in the Southern Andes of Peru. Cuzco city is a 45 min flight from Peru’s capital Lima, right in the Pacific coast. The Sacred Valley is a collection of little towns along the Urubamba river. It’s is packed with cool things to do, from hiking to adventure sports. The most famous attraction is MachuPicchu.

Flying there

To get to the Sacred Valley you have to fly in to Cuzco from Lima. Many US airlines have either one stop or direct flights from the US to Lima. As a reference, and non-stop flight from Atlanta to Lima takes about 6.5 hours.
Recommended airlines for Lima – Cuzco flights are LATAM, TACA (Avianca), LCP and Star Peru. Viva Air tends to have frequent delays.

Cusco to the Sacred Valley

Most likely your hotel can arrange airport pick up, if not, we can help you arrange private transport from the Cuzco airport to your hotel (just send us an email).
A trip from the airport to Urubamba should be between $20 to $30 for a private sedan car.
Don't rent a car unless you really are into adventure driving.

Transport and paying for stuff

Once in the Valley, it’s easy to set private transportation, or take the public bus. For short trips you can also use local moto-taxis (tuk-tuk). Private transportation is cheap and convenient for visiting the different ruins.
Although you can pay with credit card for most big purchases (hotel, nice meals, souvenirs from fancy shops), it’s better to have cash for the local services (transport, markets, street food, etc). Exchanging dollars to soles is very easy and it’s done everywhere. Current exchange is about 3.30 soles for every dollar.
